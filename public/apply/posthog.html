Cover Letter
I build agentic AI systems in production. At Coinbase, I personally built an exception triage pipeline that replaced L1 support, integrated LLM workflows that cut resolution time by 31%, and ship side projects with Claude API, n8n, and Next.js every week.
What gets me excited about PostHog is you're building the infrastructure that lets product teams actually understand their AI features. Right now everyone's shipping LLM integrations but nobody knows if they're working. PostHog is solving that gap between "we added AI" and "our AI actually improves the product."
My agentic pipeline at Coinbase does exactly what PostHog customers need to measure: it takes production exceptions, categorizes them with Claude, creates tickets, routes to the right engineer, and learns from the patterns. But here's the thing - we had zero visibility into whether the AI was making good decisions until we built custom dashboards. That's the problem PostHog solves at scale.
I have spent time on both sides. I have been the user filing bug reports and the engineer shipping fixes same-day. My side projects speak for themselves: Daily Briefing aggregates multi-source data with Claude API, ZeroToShip is Next.js + Supabase + Claude API for rapid prototyping, and Local Roots is a Go + Next.js marketplace I built from scratch.
PostHog's culture of building objectively unnecessary developer toys with dubious shareholder value is exactly why I want to work there. The best products come from engineers who can't stop building things, even when they probably should. You can see my recent experiments at brandonqueener.dev.
